Qlone Word Clock

My first microcontroller project, inspired by the Qlocktwo Clock by Biegert & Funk, was completed in 2011. It consists of an Arduino microcontroller, 114 LEDs (wired by hand, this was before the proliferation of individually-addressable LED strips!), a real-time clock, two LED drivers, and a photoresistor. The photoresistor allows the clock to dim according to the ambient light levels so that the clock doesn't blind you in the evenings.

Thanks to Flickr-users Ruud Burger and mdotliang for their photos and code to start from.
