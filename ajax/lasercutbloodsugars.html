A Year's Worth of Bloodsugars in Tangible Form

I am what you might call a "lucky" Type 1 diabetic, in that I have access to Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM), and amazing technology that is unaffordable to many diabetics around the world. A CGM uses a small probe inserted under the skin (on the backside of my arm) to measure my blood glucose value every 5 minutes and send it to my phone. This wealth of data is something that I review and act on, if necessary, at least a few times every waking hour. My artificial pancreas uses these data every 5 minutes to make adjustments to keep my "bloodsugar" within my set range.

I'm not sure if those without diabetes can really understand it, but I feel strangely close to this pile of data. Afterall, the values represent the outcome of or the possibility for me to do most of the things that make up "life"; what I eat, how I slept, when/how much/what kind of exercise I've gotten, stress, mental health, and sickness, to name a few. The importance of these data, combined with my affinity for novel data visualization methods, made we want to create a tangible representation.

Each piece of laser-cut cardstock represents one day, with the start and end times of the day and the beginning and end of the year engraved in the acrylic. In total, there are 96,352 blood glucose values included in this display measuring 5.8" x 4.5".

Perhaps you are wondering why I just finished a project in 2025 using data from 2019? This project started in 2020 (a Covid project). At that time, I pulled all the data from the MongoDB it lives in, parsed it into individual days, and began to use svgwrite (Python library) to build SVG files. I ended up stuck somewhere there and lost interest. Earlier this year (2025), I realized that I wanted to finish the project, so I sat down for a few afternoons and made it work. Laser-cutting the cardstock (16 individual sheets) in our laser cutter at work took about 3 hours, and another couple of evenings to cut the acrylic and assemble.
